TRADEMARK APPLICATION GUIDE

This guide walks through the process of filing a federal trademark application with the USPTO. While simple applications can be DIY, consult an attorney for complex marks or if you encounter issues.

---

SECTION 1: BEFORE YOU APPLY

1.1 Is Your Mark Registrable?

Strong Marks (More Protectable):
- Fanciful: Invented words (Xerox, Kodak)
- Arbitrary: Real words, unrelated to product (Apple for computers)
- Suggestive: Hints at qualities without describing (Netflix)

Weak Marks (Less Protectable or Unregistrable):
- Descriptive: Describes the product (Best Buy) - requires secondary meaning
- Generic: Common name for product (Computer for computers) - NOT registrable

1.2 Clearance Search

Before applying, search for conflicts:

Step 1: USPTO TESS Search (free)
- Go to: https://tess2.uspto.gov
- Search your exact mark
- Search phonetic equivalents
- Search in your goods/services class
- Review both live and dead marks

Step 2: State Trademark Databases
- Search formation states
- Search operating states

Step 3: Common Law Search
- Google search
- Domain name search
- Social media search
- Business name databases

Likelihood of confusion concerns: ____________________

1.3 Determine Your Filing Basis

Use in Commerce (Section 1(a)):
- You're already using the mark in commerce
- Can register mark as currently used
- Provide date of first use

Intent to Use (Section 1(b)):
- You intend to use but haven't yet
- Must file Statement of Use later
- Additional fees and steps

2.2 Mark Information

Standard Character Mark:
- Word mark without design elements
- Protects the word in any font/style/color
- Broadest protection for word marks

Design Mark (with logo/stylization):
- Includes design elements
- Specific font, color, style
- Narrower protection but protects the design

2.4 Specimens (for Use in Commerce applications)

A specimen shows your mark used in commerce:

For Goods:
- Labels, tags, packaging
- Product photos showing mark
- Website screenshot with mark on product

For Services:
- Advertising showing mark with services
- Website pages offering services under mark
- Business cards, brochures

NOT acceptable:

SECTION 3: FILE YOUR APPLICATION

3.1 Choose Filing Option

TEAS Plus ($250/class):
- Use pre-approved descriptions from ID Manual
- All communication via email
- Lowest cost

TEAS Standard ($350/class):
- Can write your own description
- More flexibility
- Higher cost

Recommendation: TEAS Plus if your goods/services fit ID Manual descriptions

3.2 Filing Process

Step 1: Create USPTO.gov account
Step 2: Start new trademark application
Step 3: Enter applicant information
Step 4: Enter mark information
Step 5: Select goods/services and class(es)
Step 6: Upload specimen (if applicable)
Step 7: Enter basis and dates
Step 8: Review and pay
Step 9: Save serial number and confirmation

SECTION 4: AFTER FILING

4.1 Timeline (approximate)

Month 0: Application filed
Month 3-6: Examining attorney assigned
Month 3-8: Office action (if issues) OR approval
Month 8-12: Publication in Official Gazette
Month 9-13: Registration (if no opposition)

Intent to Use Additional Steps:
- Notice of Allowance issued
- 6 months to file Statement of Use (extensions available)
- Registration after SOU approved

4.2 Responding to Office Actions

Common Issues:
- Likelihood of confusion with prior mark
- Descriptive mark (needs disclaimer or evidence)
- Specimen issues
- Description issues

Response deadline: 3 months (can extend 3 more months for fee)

If you receive Office Action:
- [ ] Read carefully
- [ ] Note deadline
- [ ] Consider attorney consultation
- [ ] Respond within deadline

4.3 Monitoring and Maintenance

After filing:
- [ ] Monitor status via TSDR (tsdr.uspto.gov)
- [ ] Respond to any office actions
- [ ] Watch for publication date

After registration:
- [ ] Year 5-6: File Section 8 Declaration + Section 15 (incontestability)
- [ ] Year 9-10: File Section 8 + Section 9 Renewal
- [ ] Every 10 years: Renew

---

SECTION 5: COST SUMMARY

Initial Filing:
- TEAS Plus: $250/class
- TEAS Standard: $350/class

If Intent to Use:
- Statement of Use: $100/class
- Extension: $125/class (up to 5)

Maintenance:
- Section 8 (Year 5-6): $225/class
- Section 15 (Year 5-6): $200/class
- Section 8 + 9 (Year 9-10): $525/class

Total estimated cost (Use in Commerce, 1 class): ~$250-350 initially
Total estimated cost (Intent to Use, 1 class): ~$350-600+ total

---

SECTION 6: WHEN TO HIRE AN ATTORNEY

Consider professional help if:
- [ ] Clearance search found potential conflicts
- [ ] Your mark is descriptive or has design elements
- [ ] You receive an Office Action
- [ ] Someone opposes your application
- [ ] You're filing in multiple classes
- [ ] International protection is needed
- [ ] You're unsure about any step
