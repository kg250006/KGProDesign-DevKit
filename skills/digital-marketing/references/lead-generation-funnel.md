<overview>
Lead generation funnel structure for building email lists and nurturing prospects into customers.
</overview>

<funnel_structure>

<stage name="traffic">
Goal: Drive targeted visitors to landing page

Sources:
- Organic content (SEO, social media)
- Paid advertising (Facebook, Google, LinkedIn)
- Partnerships (guest posts, podcast appearances)
- Referrals (word of mouth)
- Direct outreach (email, LinkedIn)

Key metric: Cost per click / Visitor volume
</stage>

<stage name="opt-in">
Goal: Convert visitors to subscribers

Components:
- Landing page with compelling offer
- Lead magnet (valuable free content)
- Opt-in form (email capture)
- Thank you page (delivery + next step)

Key metric: Opt-in rate (target: 20-40%)
</stage>

<stage name="nurture">
Goal: Build relationship and demonstrate value

Components:
- Welcome email sequence (5-7 emails)
- Ongoing newsletter (weekly/bi-weekly)
- Value-first content
- Soft pitch opportunities

Key metric: Email engagement (opens, clicks)
</stage>

<stage name="conversion">
Goal: Convert subscribers to customers

Components:
- Sales email sequence
- Sales page or offer page
- Checkout process
- Follow-up for non-buyers

Key metric: Sales conversion rate
</stage>

</funnel_structure>

<landing_page_elements>

<element name="headline">
Purpose: Capture attention, communicate value
Format: Clear promise or benefit
Example: "The 5-Step System to Double Your Email Open Rates"
</element>

<element name="subheadline">
Purpose: Expand on headline, add context
Format: Secondary benefit or for whom
Example: "Perfect for coaches and consultants who want more engaged subscribers"
</element>

<element name="benefit-bullets">
Purpose: Showcase what they'll get
Format: 3-5 specific outcomes
Example:
- Learn the subject line formula that gets 40%+ open rates
- Discover the best day and time to send your emails
- Get 10 proven email templates you can copy
</element>

<element name="social-proof">
Purpose: Build trust and credibility
Options:
- Testimonials from past subscribers
- Number of subscribers ("Join 15,000+ marketers")
- Brand logos (as seen in)
- Expert credentials
</element>

<element name="opt-in-form">
Purpose: Capture email address
Best practices:
- Minimal fields (email only to start)
- Compelling button text ("Get Free Guide" not "Submit")
- Above the fold placement
- Mobile-friendly
</element>

<element name="cta-button">
Purpose: Drive action
Format: Action-oriented text
Examples: "Download Now", "Get Instant Access", "Send Me the Guide"
</element>

</landing_page_elements>

<lead_magnet_types>

<type name="checklist">
Description: Step-by-step list for completing a task
Conversion: High
Example: "The Ultimate Product Launch Checklist"
Best for: Action-takers who want quick wins
</type>

<type name="template">
Description: Fill-in-the-blank or customizable resource
Conversion: High
Example: "10 High-Converting Email Subject Line Templates"
Best for: People who want done-for-you solutions
</type>

<type name="guide-ebook">
Description: Comprehensive resource on a topic
Conversion: Medium
Example: "The Complete Guide to Facebook Advertising"
Best for: Building authority, problem-solvers
</type>

<type name="video-training">
Description: Video content teaching a skill
Conversion: Medium
Example: "Free Masterclass: Build Your First Funnel"
Best for: Visual learners, building personal connection
</type>

<type name="quiz">
Description: Interactive assessment with personalized results
Conversion: Very High
Example: "What's Your Marketing Personality Type?"
Best for: Segmentation, engagement, curiosity
</type>

<type name="tool-calculator">
Description: Practical tool that solves a problem
Conversion: High
Example: "ROI Calculator for Your Marketing Spend"
Best for: Ongoing value, repeat visits
</type>

</lead_magnet_types>

<email_sequences>

<sequence name="welcome">
Purpose: Deliver lead magnet, introduce yourself, set expectations

Email 1 (Immediate): Deliver
- Thank them for signing up
- Deliver the lead magnet
- Set expectations for future emails
- Quick tip to get started

Email 2 (Day 1): Story
- Share your background/story
- Why you care about this topic
- Build personal connection

Email 3 (Day 3): Value
- Actionable tip related to lead magnet
- Demonstrate expertise
- Link to helpful content

Email 4 (Day 5): Social proof
- Case study or testimonial
- What's possible with your help
- Results others have achieved

Email 5 (Day 7): Soft pitch
- Introduction to paid offering
- How it helps them further
- Low-pressure invitation
</sequence>

</email_sequences>

<optimization>

<test name="landing-page">
Elements to test:
- Headlines
- Lead magnet format
- Button copy
- Form length
- Social proof placement
- Page length

How to test:
- A/B test one element at a time
- Minimum 100 conversions per variant
- Statistical significance before declaring winner
</test>

<test name="traffic">
Elements to test:
- Ad copy and creative
- Audience targeting
- Traffic sources
- Keywords (for search)

How to test:
- Compare cost per opt-in across sources
- Scale what works, cut what doesn't
</test>

<test name="email">
Elements to test:
- Subject lines
- Sender name
- Send times
- Email length
- CTA placement

How to test:
- A/B test in email platform
- Compare open and click rates
</test>

</optimization>
