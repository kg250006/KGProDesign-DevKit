<section name="content-pillars">

Define your core themes:

<pillar_criteria>
- Aligned with audience interests and pain points
- Connected to your products/services
- Differentiates you from competitors
- Sustainable to create content around
- Searchable (has SEO potential)
</pillar_criteria>

2. Tactics: Specific channel how-tos, tools, techniques
3. Case Studies: Client results, industry examples

5. Behind-the-Scenes: Team, culture, process

<example business="saas-product">

<section name="content-types">

<type name="blog-posts">
Purpose: SEO, education, authority building
Length: 1,500-3,000 words for SEO
Frequency: 2-4 per month minimum

Post types:
- How-to guides (step-by-step instructions)
- Listicles (X ways to..., X tools for...)
- Ultimate guides (comprehensive resource)
- Case studies (real results)
- Comparisons (X vs Y)
- News/commentary (industry updates)
- Interviews (expert insights)
</type>

<type name="video-content">
Purpose: Engagement, trust-building, YouTube SEO
Length: 5-15 minutes (YouTube), 15-60 seconds (short-form)

Video types:
- Tutorials and how-tos
- Explainers and overviews
- Interviews and podcasts
- Behind-the-scenes
- Webinars and presentations
- Product demos
- Testimonials
</type>

<type name="podcasts">
Purpose: Thought leadership, relationship building, reach
Length: 20-60 minutes

Episode types:
- Solo episodes (your expertise)
- Interviews (guest expertise)
- Co-hosted discussions
- Q&A from audience
- Case study deep-dives
</type>

<type name="lead-magnets">
Purpose: Email list building, lead generation
Formats: PDFs, videos, templates, tools

Popular formats:
- Checklists and cheat sheets
- Templates and swipe files
- Ebooks and guides
- Video courses
- Quizzes and assessments
- Tools and calculators
</type>

<type name="social-content">
Purpose: Awareness, engagement, traffic
Length: Short-form, platform-specific

Content types:
- Text posts
- Images and graphics
- Carousels
- Short-form video
- Stories
- Live streams
</type>

<seo_content>

<section name="keyword-research">

<process>
1. Brainstorm seed keywords (what would your audience search?)
2. Use tools to expand (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Ubersuggest, free: Google Keyword Planner)
3. Analyze search volume and difficulty
4. Check search intent (informational, transactional, navigational)
5. Prioritize keywords by:
   - Relevance to your business
   - Search volume
   - Difficulty to rank
   - Commercial intent
</process>

<keyword_types>
Head terms: High volume, high competition (1-2 words)
Long-tail: Lower volume, lower competition (3+ words)
Questions: How, what, why, where questions

Strategy: Target long-tail keywords first, build up to head terms
</keyword_types>

</section>

<section name="on-page-seo">

<optimization_checklist>
- Target keyword in title (near beginning)
- Target keyword in URL
- Target keyword in H1 (only one H1)
- Target keyword in first 100 words
- Related keywords in H2s and content
- Meta description with keyword (150-160 chars)
- Image alt text with keywords
- Internal links to related content
- External links to authoritative sources
</optimization_checklist>

<content_structure>
- Compelling title with target keyword
- Clear introduction that hooks reader
- Table of contents for long posts
- H2/H3 headers with keyword variations
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Bullet points and numbered lists
- Images, graphics, or video
- Strong conclusion with CTA
</content_structure>

</section>

<section name="content-upgrades">
Lead magnets specific to individual posts:
- Checklist version of how-to post
- Template mentioned in article
- Extended version with bonus tips
- Video walkthrough of process
- Printable PDF summary

Implementation:
- Add opt-in form within content
- Promise additional value
- Deliver immediately via email
- Tag subscribers by content interest
</section>

</seo_content>

Create once, distribute many:

Blog post becomes:
- Email newsletter
- LinkedIn article
- Twitter thread
- Instagram carousel
- YouTube video
- Podcast episode
- Infographic
- SlideShare presentation

System:
1. Create "pillar" content (long-form)
2. Extract key points
3. Reformat for each platform
4. Schedule distribution
5. Update and recycle top performers
</section>
