<overview>
Email marketing playbook covering list building, automation, segmentation, and deliverability optimization.
</overview>

<fundamentals>

<section name="list-building">

<method name="lead-magnets">
Value-first opt-in offers:
- Checklists and cheat sheets (highest conversion)
- Templates and swipe files
- Guides and ebooks
- Video training
- Quizzes and assessments
- Free tools and calculators
- Resource libraries

Best practices:
- Solve one specific problem
- Deliver immediate value
- Promise clear outcome
- Match to paid offering
</method>

<method name="opt-in-forms">
Form placement:
- Dedicated landing pages
- Homepage above fold
- Blog post content upgrades
- Exit intent popups
- Sidebar forms
- Footer signup

Form optimization:
- Clear headline with benefit
- Minimal fields (email only to start)
- Compelling CTA button text
- Social proof near form
- Mobile-friendly design
</method>

<method name="lead-sources">
Where to get subscribers:
- Website traffic (organic and paid)
- Social media profiles and posts
- Webinars and live events
- Partner cross-promotions
- Podcast appearances
- Guest content
- Physical events
</method>

</section>

<section name="email-sequences">

<sequence name="welcome-sequence">
Purpose: Introduce, build relationship, set expectations
Length: 5-7 emails over 7-10 days

Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver lead magnet + welcome
- Thank them for subscribing
- Deliver promised content
- Set expectations for future emails
- Quick story or intro

Email 2 (Day 1-2): Your story
- Why you do what you do
- Your journey and credibility
- Connect on personal level

Email 3 (Day 3-4): Quick win
- Actionable tip they can use today
- Demonstrate expertise
- Build trust through value

Email 4 (Day 5-6): Social proof
- Case study or testimonial
- Results others have achieved
- Possibility thinking

Email 5 (Day 7-8): Soft pitch
- Introduce your offering
- How it helps them further
- Low-pressure invitation
</sequence>

<sequence name="nurture-sequence">
Purpose: Maintain relationship, provide value, stay top of mind
Format: Weekly or bi-weekly newsletter

Content types:
- Educational tips and how-tos
- Industry news and commentary
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Case studies and examples
- Curated resources
- Personal stories and lessons
</sequence>

<sequence name="sales-sequence">
Purpose: Convert subscribers to customers
Length: 5-7 emails over 5-10 days

Email 1: Problem agitation
- Describe their current pain
- What happens if nothing changes
- They're not alone

Email 2: Solution introduction
- Your approach to solving it
- Why this method works
- Preview of transformation

Email 3: Case study/proof
- Specific client results
- Before and after
- Testimonial

Email 4: Objection handling
- Address top concerns
- FAQ format works well
- Remove barriers

Email 5: Deadline/urgency
- Limited time or quantity
- What they'll miss
- Last chance to act

Email 6-7: Final reminder
- Cart closing soon
- Recap all value
- Strong CTA
</sequence>

</section>

- Visited specific pages

- Product category interests

<segments name="demographic">
Based on who they are:
- Location
- Industry
- Company size
- Job role
- Budget
</segments>

<segments name="lifecycle">
Based on customer journey:
- New subscriber
- Active lead
- Customer
- Repeat customer

<best_practices>

<practice name="subject-lines">
Writing high-open subjects:
- Curiosity (incomplete loops)
- Benefit-driven (what's in it for them)
- Urgency (time-sensitive)
- Personalization (their name or interests)
- Questions (engage the brain)

Length: 30-50 characters ideal
Test: A/B test subjects regularly
Avoid: Spam triggers, ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation!!!
</practice>

<practice name="preview-text">
Optimize the preview:
- Extends the subject line
- Adds context or intrigue
- Not just the first line of email
- 40-90 characters
</practice>

<practice name="email-copy">
Writing engaging emails:
- One main idea per email
- Conversational tone
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Clear call to action
- Mobile-friendly formatting
- PS line for second hook
</practice>

<practice name="timing">
When to send:
- Test for your audience
- Generally: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM or 1-3 PM
- Avoid Monday morning and Friday afternoon
- Consider time zones
- Consistency matters more than perfection
</practice>

<practice name="frequency">
How often to email:
- Minimum: 1x per week
- Maximum: Daily (if value justifies)
- Consistency is key
- Set expectations in welcome email
- Monitor unsubscribes for warning signs
</practice>

</best_practices>

<deliverability>

<factors name="sender-reputation">
What affects reputation:
- Bounce rates (keep under 2%)
- Spam complaints (keep under 0.1%)
- Engagement rates (opens, clicks)
- List hygiene
- Authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
</factors>

<factors name="list-hygiene">
Keep list clean:
- Remove hard bounces immediately
- Re-engage or remove unengaged after 90 days
- Honor unsubscribes promptly
- Never buy lists
- Use double opt-in for quality
</factors>

<factors name="technical-setup">
Authentication:
- SPF record: Authorize sending servers
- DKIM: Verify email isn't altered
- DMARC: Policy for failed authentication
- Custom sending domain recommended
</factors>

<factors name="avoiding-spam-filters">
Best practices:
- Avoid spam trigger words
- Balance text to image ratio
- Include unsubscribe link
- Use recognizable from name
- Don't use URL shorteners
- Test before sending
</factors>

</deliverability>

<metrics>

<metric name="open-rate">
Definition: Emails opened / emails delivered
Benchmark: 20-30% (varies by industry)
Improve: Subject lines, sender name, timing
</metric>

<metric name="click-rate">
Definition: Clicks / emails delivered
Benchmark: 2-5%
Improve: Copy, CTA, link placement
</metric>

<metric name="click-to-open-rate">
Definition: Clicks / emails opened
Benchmark: 10-15%
Improve: Content relevance, CTA strength
</metric>

<metric name="unsubscribe-rate">
Definition: Unsubscribes / emails delivered
Benchmark: Under 0.5%
Warning sign: Over 1% per email
</metric>

<metric name="list-growth-rate">
Definition: (New subscribers - Unsubscribes) / Total list
Goal: Positive growth month over month
</metric>

<metric name="revenue-per-email">
Definition: Revenue attributed to email / emails sent
Track: By campaign and sequence
</metric>

</metrics>
