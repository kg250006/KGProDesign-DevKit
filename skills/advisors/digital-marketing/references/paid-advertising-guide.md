<platform name="facebook-instagram-ads">

<campaign_structure>
Campaign Level: Objective (awareness, traffic, conversions)
Ad Set Level: Targeting, budget, placement, schedule
Ad Level: Creative, copy, CTA
</campaign_structure>

<objectives>
Awareness:
- Brand awareness (reach people likely to remember)
- Reach (maximize people who see ad)

Consideration:
- Traffic (send people to website)
- Engagement (get likes, comments, shares)
- Video views (get people to watch)
- Lead generation (collect leads in-platform)

Conversion:
- Conversions (drive website actions)
- Catalog sales (e-commerce)
- Store traffic (local business)
</objectives>

<targeting_options>
Core Audiences:
- Demographics (age, gender, location, language)
- Interests (based on likes, activities)
- Behaviors (purchase behavior, device usage)

Custom Audiences:
- Website visitors (pixel required)
- Email list uploads
- App users
- Engagement (video viewers, page engagers)

Lookalike Audiences:
- Based on custom audiences
- 1% = most similar, larger % = broader
- Recommend starting with 1-3%
</targeting_options>

<creative_best_practices>
Images:
- 1080x1080 (square) or 1200x628 (landscape)
- Minimal text (under 20%)
- Eye-catching, thumb-stopping
- Brand colors but not too logo-heavy

Video:
- 15-30 seconds optimal
- Square or vertical for mobile
- Captions for silent viewing
- Hook in first 3 seconds

Copy:
- Lead with benefit or curiosity
- Short paragraphs
- Clear CTA
- Emojis OK in moderation
- Test multiple versions
</creative_best_practices>

<optimization_tactics>
- Start with broad targeting, narrow based on data
- Test 2-4 ad variations minimum
- Kill underperformers after 1000 impressions
- Scale winners by 20% budget increases
- Refresh creative every 2-4 weeks
- Use Advantage+ placements (let AI optimize)
</optimization_tactics>

</platform>

<platform name="google-ads">

<campaign_types>
Search: Text ads on Google search results
Display: Image/video ads across websites
Shopping: Product listings for e-commerce
Video: Ads on YouTube
Performance Max: AI-driven across all placements
</campaign_types>

<search_ads>
Keyword match types:
- Broad match: keyword (widest reach)
- Phrase match: "keyword" (moderate targeting)
- Exact match: [keyword] (precise targeting)

Ad structure:
- 3 headlines (30 chars each)
- 2 descriptions (90 chars each)
- Display URL path
- Extensions (sitelinks, callouts, etc.)

Quality Score factors:
- Expected CTR
- Ad relevance
- Landing page experience
- Improve all three for lower costs
</search_ads>

<bidding_strategies>
Manual CPC: You set max bid per click
Maximize clicks: Get most clicks within budget
Target CPA: AI optimizes for cost per acquisition
Target ROAS: AI optimizes for return on ad spend
Maximize conversions: Get most conversions within budget

Recommendation: Start manual or maximize clicks, move to target CPA after 30+ conversions
</bidding_strategies>

<optimization_tactics>
- Add negative keywords to eliminate waste
- Use ad extensions to improve CTR
- A/B test ad copy continuously
- Improve landing page relevance
- Check search terms report weekly
- Adjust bids by device, location, time
</optimization_tactics>

<fundamental_concepts>

<section name="campaign-structure">
Account → Campaigns → Ad Groups/Sets → Ads

Best practices:
- Organize by objective or product
- Separate audiences into ad sets
- Test multiple creatives per ad set
- Use consistent naming conventions
</section>

<section name="budgeting">
Daily vs Lifetime:
- Daily: Consistent spend each day
- Lifetime: Flexible, optimized by platform

Budget allocation:
- 70% to proven performers
- 20% to optimization/testing
- 10% to experimental

Starting budgets:
- Facebook/Instagram: $10-20/day minimum
- Google Search: $20-50/day minimum
- LinkedIn: $30-50/day minimum
</section>

<section name="testing-framework">
What to test:
- Audiences (different targeting)
- Creative (images, video, copy)
- Offers (different CTAs, angles)
- Landing pages (different versions)

Testing rules:
- One variable at a time
- Sufficient sample size (1000+ impressions)
- Statistical significance
- Document and apply learnings
</section>

<section name="metrics">
Awareness metrics:
- Impressions
- Reach
- Frequency
- CPM (cost per 1000 impressions)

Engagement metrics:
- CTR (click-through rate)
- CPC (cost per click)
- Engagement rate

Conversion metrics:
- Conversions
- CPA (cost per acquisition)
- ROAS (return on ad spend)
- Conversion rate

Benchmarks vary by industry:
- Facebook CTR: 0.9-1.5%
- Google Search CTR: 2-5%
- LinkedIn CTR: 0.3-0.6%
</section>

<section name="retargeting">
Audiences to retarget:
- Website visitors (all, specific pages)
- Cart abandoners
- Video viewers
- Email subscribers
- Past customers

Strategies:
- Remind of value proposition
- Handle objections
- Offer incentive (discount, bonus)
- Create urgency (limited time)
- Show social proof

Timing:
- 1-3 days: Show same offer
- 4-14 days: Different angle
- 14-30 days: Last chance offer
- 30+ days: Reduce frequency
</section>

</fundamental_concepts>

<optimization_playbook>

<phase name="launch">
Days 1-3:
- Let ads run with sufficient budget
- Don't make changes too quickly
- Gather initial data
- Watch for obvious issues
</phase>

<phase name="learning">
Days 4-14:
- Analyze performance by ad, audience
- Identify top performers
- Pause clear underperformers
- Add negative keywords (search)
</phase>

<phase name="optimization">
Ongoing:
- Scale winners (increase budget 20% at a time)
- Test new variations
- Refresh creative before fatigue
- Expand targeting based on learnings
- Improve landing pages
</phase>

</optimization_playbook>
