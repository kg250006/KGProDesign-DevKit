<overview>
Industry-specific legal guidance for e-commerce businesses selling physical or digital products.
</overview>

<regulatory_landscape>

<regulation name="consumer-protection">
FTC Act:
- Prohibition on unfair or deceptive practices
- Truth in advertising requirements
- Clear pricing disclosure
- Endorsement and testimonial guidelines

State Consumer Protection:
- State-specific unfair trade practice laws
- Varying remedies and enforcement
- Class action exposure
</regulation>

<regulation name="product-safety">
Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC):
- Mandatory reporting of safety issues
- Product recall requirements
- Children's product certification (CPSIA)
- Labeling requirements

FDA (food, cosmetics, supplements):
- Good Manufacturing Practices
- Labeling requirements
- Health claims restrictions
- Registration requirements
</regulation>

<regulation name="online-commerce-laws">
ROSCA (Restore Online Shoppers' Confidence Act):
- Clear disclosure of terms before charging
- Express informed consent for recurring charges
- Simple cancellation mechanism

Electronic Signatures (E-SIGN, UETA):
- Electronic contracts enforceable
- Consumer consent for electronic records
- Retention requirements
</regulation>

<regulation name="sales-tax">
Nexus Rules (post-Wayfair):
- Economic nexus in most states
- Typical thresholds: $100K sales or 200 transactions
- Remote seller obligations
- Marketplace facilitator laws

Compliance:
- Registration in nexus states
- Collection at point of sale
- Remittance by deadline
- Record keeping
</regulation>

</regulatory_landscape>

<essential_agreements>

<agreement name="terms-of-sale">
Essential Terms:
- Product descriptions and specifications
- Pricing and payment terms
- Shipping and delivery
- Returns and refunds
- Warranties and disclaimers
- Limitation of liability
- Dispute resolution

Consumer-Facing Considerations:
- Plain language requirement
- Conspicuous disclosure of material terms
- Click-wrap or browse-wrap implementation
</agreement>

<agreement name="return-policy">
Legal Requirements:
- Must honor stated policy
- Some states require conspicuous posting
- Specific rules for certain products (mattresses, swimwear)

Best Practices:
- Clear timeframe for returns
- Condition requirements
- Refund method (original payment, store credit)
- Who pays return shipping
- Restocking fees (if any)
- Non-returnable items
</agreement>

<agreement name="shipping-policy">
FTC Mail Order Rule:
- Ship within stated timeframe or 30 days
- Notify customer of delays
- Offer to cancel for refund
- First-class mail refund within 7 days of cancellation

Policy Contents:
- Processing time vs. shipping time
- Shipping methods and costs
- Delivery estimates
- International shipping terms
- Risk of loss transfer
- Carrier liability limitations
</agreement>

<agreement name="privacy-policy">
E-commerce Specific:
- Payment information handling
- Browsing and purchase history
- Marketing communications consent
- Third-party analytics and advertising
- Social login data
- Review and rating data
</agreement>

<agreement name="supplier-agreements">
Key Terms:
- Product specifications
- Quality requirements
- Pricing and payment
- Minimum order quantities
- Lead times
- Defect handling
- IP rights (especially for private label)
- Compliance with laws
- Insurance requirements
</agreement>

</essential_agreements>

<intellectual_property>

<ip_consideration name="product-images">
Ownership: Who created or owns the images?
Rights Needed: Right to use for marketing, resale, modification
Supplier Images: Get written license
User-Generated: Terms for customer photos/reviews
Third-Party Content: License or fair use analysis
</ip_consideration>

<ip_consideration name="product-descriptions">
Original Content: You own your descriptions
Manufacturer Descriptions: May be shared with retailers
SEO Considerations: Unique descriptions prevent duplicate content
Accuracy: Avoid false or misleading claims
</ip_consideration>

<ip_consideration name="brand-protection">
Trademark: Register your brand name and logo
Domain: Secure relevant domains
Counterfeit Prevention: Brand registry programs (Amazon, etc.)
Gray Market: Authorized reseller agreements
</ip_consideration>

<ip_consideration name="selling-branded-goods">
First Sale Doctrine: Generally can resell authentic goods
Limitations: May not modify, repackage without permission
Advertising: Can use brand name for truthful advertising
Authorized Reseller: May have contractual restrictions
</ip_consideration>

</intellectual_property>

<liability_management>

<liability_area name="product-liability">
Strict Liability: Liable for defective products regardless of fault
Negligence: Failure to exercise reasonable care
Breach of Warranty: Failing to meet express or implied promises

Protections:
- Product liability insurance
- Supplier indemnification
- Quality control procedures
- Clear product warnings
- Warranty limitations (where permitted)
</liability_area>

<liability_area name="advertising-claims">
FTC Requirements:
- Truthful and not misleading
- Evidence to back up claims
- Clear disclosure of material connections
- Endorsement guidelines

High-Risk Claims:
- Health or safety claims
- Made in USA claims
- Environmental claims (greenwashing)
- Comparative advertising
</liability_area>

<liability_area name="third-party-content">
Customer Reviews:
- Generally protected by CDA Section 230
- Must remove if defamatory upon notice (some argue)
- FTC requires disclosure of incentivized reviews
- Cannot manipulate or fake reviews

User-Generated Content:
- IP infringement risk
- Terms granting license to use
- DMCA takedown procedures
</liability_area>

<liability_area name="marketplace-liability">
If Operating Marketplace:
- Generally protected as intermediary
- Must respond to IP complaints
- May have product safety obligations
- Varying state laws on marketplace facilitator liability
</liability_area>

</liability_management>

<payment_processing>

<consideration name="pci-compliance">
Most E-commerce: SAQ A (using hosted payment page)
Requirements:
- Use PCI-compliant payment processor
- Don't store card data on your servers
- Secure connection (HTTPS)
- Annual SAQ submission
</consideration>

<consideration name="payment-disputes">
Chargeback Process:
- Respond within timeframe (usually 7-14 days)
- Provide evidence of delivery, authorization
- Track chargeback rates (1% threshold)

Prevention:
- Clear billing descriptor
- Accurate product descriptions
- Responsive customer service
- Delivery confirmation
- Fraud screening tools
</consideration>

<consideration name="buy-now-pay-later">
Regulatory Consideration: CFPB scrutiny increasing
Disclosure: Must clearly explain terms
Risk: Higher return rates, potential disputes
Terms: Review BNPL provider agreement carefully
</consideration>

</payment_processing>

<international_commerce>

<consideration name="cross-border-sales">
Customs and Duties:
- Determine who pays (DDP vs. DDU)
- De minimis thresholds vary by country
- HS code classification
- Restricted products check

Consumer Protection:
- EU Consumer Rights Directive (14-day returns)
- UK Consumer Contracts Regulations
- Local warranty requirements
</consideration>

<consideration name="gdpr-for-ecommerce">
If Selling to EU:
- Consent for marketing
- Privacy policy in local language
- Data subject rights
- Representative in EU (if no establishment)
- Standard contractual clauses for transfers
</consideration>

</international_commerce>

<common_pitfalls>

<pitfall name="drip-pricing">
Issue: Adding fees during checkout
Solution: All-inclusive pricing or clear upfront disclosure
Risk: FTC enforcement, consumer complaints
</pitfall>

<pitfall name="fake-scarcity">
Issue: False "only 3 left" or fake countdown timers
Solution: Only display accurate inventory/time limits
Risk: FTC deceptive practices violation
</pitfall>

<pitfall name="review-manipulation">
Issue: Fake reviews, undisclosed paid reviews
Solution: Follow FTC endorsement guidelines
Risk: FTC enforcement, platform bans
</pitfall>

<pitfall name="subscription-traps">
Issue: Difficult cancellation, hidden recurring charges
Solution: Clear disclosure, easy cancellation
Risk: FTC enforcement, ROSCA violations, state AG actions
</pitfall>

</common_pitfalls>
